Accelerep ("Accelerep," "we," "our," or "us") provides a B2B SaaS sales pipeline and CRM platform accessible at salespipelinetracker.com (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, with whom we share it, and what rights you have with respect to it.

By accessing or using the Service, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Service.

1

Who This Policy Applies To

This policy applies to:

  • Organizational administrators ("Admins") who create and manage an Accelerep account on behalf of a business;
  • Sales managers and sales representatives ("Users") who are invited into an organization's Accelerep workspace; and
  • Visitors to our website or marketing pages who have not yet signed up.

Accelerep is a business-to-business (B2B) platform and is not directed at consumers or individuals under the age of 18. If you believe a minor has provided us with personal data, please contact us immediately.

2

Information We Collect

2.1 Account & Identity Information

When an organization signs up for Accelerep, we collect:

  • Name and email address of the account administrator
  • Organization name
  • Account credentials managed through our authentication provider, Clerk
  • User roles assigned within your organization (Admin, Manager, Sales Rep, Read-Only)

2.2 CRM & Pipeline Data

As you use the Service, you and your team enter and store business data, including:

  • Sales opportunities, pipeline stages, deal values, and close dates
  • Account records (company names, industry, parent/sub-account relationships)
  • Contact records (names, email addresses, phone numbers, job titles)
  • Lead records and lead source information
  • Tasks, activities, and notes
  • Quotes and line-item pricing data
  • Commission plans, quota targets, and performance data

This data belongs to your organization. We process it solely to provide the Service.

2.3 Calendar & Integration Data

If you connect Google Calendar, we access calendar event metadata (titles, times, attendees) through the Google Calendar API to display and create events within the Service. We store OAuth tokens necessary to maintain that connection. We do not access calendar data beyond what is required for the Service features you use.

2.4 Usage & Log Data

We automatically collect technical data when you access the Service:

  • IP address and browser/device information
  • Pages visited, features accessed, and timestamps
  • Error logs and performance diagnostics

This data is used to operate, maintain, and improve the Service.

2.5 Communications

If you contact us for support or send us an email, we retain those communications to resolve your request and improve our support quality.

3

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the Service for your organization
  • Authenticate users and enforce organization-level access controls
  • Send transactional emails (e.g., task reminders, pipeline alerts, digest notifications) via Resend
  • Send SMS notifications where you have configured and opted in to that feature via Twilio
  • Sync with Google Calendar when you have authorized that integration
  • Generate PDF documents (e.g., approved quotes) on your behalf
  • Analyze usage patterns to improve features and fix bugs
  • Respond to support requests and communicate Service updates
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and prevent fraud or abuse

Legal Bases for Processing (EEA/UK Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Contract: Processing necessary to provide the Service under our agreement with your organization.
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing for fraud prevention, security, and Service improvement, where those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal Obligation: Processing required to comply with applicable law.
  • Consent: Where we have obtained your consent (e.g., for optional communications), you may withdraw it at any time.
5

Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell your personal data. We share data only as described below.

5.1 Sub-Processors & Service Providers

We engage the following sub-processors to operate the Service:

Authentication
Clerk — manages user identity, organization membership, and session tokens.
Database
Neon — provides PostgreSQL database infrastructure on which your organization's data is stored.
Hosting
Netlify — hosts the web application and serverless backend functions.
Email
Resend — delivers transactional emails on your behalf.
SMS
Twilio — delivers SMS notifications where configured.
Calendar
Google — processes calendar data when you authorize the Google Calendar integration.

Each sub-processor is contractually bound to process data only on our instructions and to maintain appropriate security measures.

5.2 Within Your Organization

Data you enter is visible to other members of your Accelerep organization according to the role-based access controls you configure. Admins can view all data; Managers can view data for their team; Sales Reps can view only their own data by default.

5.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or governmental authority, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Accelerep, our customers, or the public.

5.4 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify affected organizations and honor the commitments made in this policy.

6

Data Retention

We retain your organization's data for as long as your account is active. If you cancel or terminate your account, we will delete your data within 90 days unless we are legally required to retain it longer.

Usage logs and aggregated analytics may be retained for up to 24 months for security and product improvement purposes.

7

Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data, including:

  • All data transmitted between your browser and the Service is encrypted via TLS/HTTPS.
  • Authentication tokens are verified on every request using Clerk's cryptographically signed JWTs.
  • All database queries are scoped to your organization's unique identifier, ensuring strict tenant isolation.
  • Access to production infrastructure is restricted to authorized personnel.

No security measure is perfect. While we work to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security. Please notify us immediately at the contact address below if you suspect a security incident.

8

Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request that we correct inaccurate data.
  • Deletion: Request that we delete your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Portability: Request your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Restriction / Objection: Object to or request restriction of certain processing.
  • Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

Organization Admins can exercise many of these rights directly within the Service (Settings → Security & Data). For additional requests, contact us at the address below. We will respond within 30 days.

9

Cookies & Local Storage

Accelerep uses browser localStorage to cache certain non-sensitive settings (such as your last active tab) for a faster experience. We do not use third-party advertising cookies. Our authentication provider, Clerk, may set session cookies necessary to maintain your login state.

You can clear localStorage at any time through your browser's developer tools or privacy settings. Doing so will not affect your data stored in the Accelerep database.

We rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) where required by applicable law.
